Ablar village belongs to Kulikam village and is located 3 km to the northeast of the village. One of the first mentions of the medieval village in written sources dates back to 1595, as a village free from rayas and paying a tax of 2,500 points. The half-ruined, one-nave church (11.50-6.11m) and the cemetery have been preserved in the Ablar area. In the latter, there are pedestals of quadrangular pillars and ram-shaped tombstones.
